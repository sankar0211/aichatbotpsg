What programs and courses does PSG College of Technology offer? PSG Tech offers a wide range of programs. Undergraduates can enroll in 4‑year B.E./B.Tech degrees in fields like Automobile, Biomedical, Civil, CSE (including an AI&ML track), EEE, ECE, Instrumentation, Mechanical, Metallurgy, Production, Robotics, Biotechnology, Fashion, IT, and Textile Technology (psgtech.edu). How do I get admitted to PSG Tech for undergraduate (B.E./B.Tech) and postgraduate (M.E./M.Tech/MCA) programs? Admission follows Tamil Nadu government and Anna University norms. For B.E./B.Tech, candidates must have completed 10+2 (with required marks) and admission is through the single-window counseling conducted by the Directorate of Technical Education (DoTE), Chennai (TNEA). For M.E./M.Tech, candidates need a relevant bachelor’s degree and go through Anna University’s counseling process, MCA admissions follow DoTE counseling. Entrance exams (like TANCET for MCA/MBA or GATE for M.Tech) are required as per university rules. Official announcements appear in newspapers or on DoTE/Anna University websites each year (Contact the Admissions Office for any clarifications)
Are there management-quota or lateral-entry seats at PSG Tech? Yes. For postgraduate programs (M.E./M.Tech and MCA), PSG Tech invites “expressions of interest” for management-quota seats. These seats require a separate application and higher fees/donations beyond the regular counseling seats. For undergraduates, a limited number of self‑financing (management/donation) seats are also available, but fees are significantly higher (often several lakhs) and details are published by the college during counseling. PSG Tech also admits diploma holders directly into the second year of B.E./B.Tech through lateral-entry counseling. Lateral-entry candidates must submit their diploma certificates and other documents at the time of admission. Upon provisional allotment, they pay tuition fees by Demand Draft to the college (no cash). (For example, in 2022 a diploma-holder in an aided branch paid ~₹19,865.)
What is the fee structure and how do I pay fees? Fees vary by program and category. Government-aided courses have relatively low tuition (for instance, B.Sc. Applied Science is about ₹20,000 per year). First-year tuition for B.E./B.Tech (aided seats) is on the order of ₹50–60 thousand. Self‑financing and management seats are much higher. Hostel and mess charges are extra (see Hostel). Fees are typically paid via bank challan/online transfer or Demand Draft only; cash is not accepted. (Students should use the college’s prescribed payment modes and meet payment deadlines.)
